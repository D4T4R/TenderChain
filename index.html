India's Public Procurement Challenge

How blockchain is revolutionizing government tender management in India

Paper-based Tender Issues

Despite digital initiatives, 76% of Indian government procurement still relies on paper documentation at some stage of the process, creating opportunities for manipulation and corruption.

Manual verification processes delay project timelines by an average of 112 days per tender.

Physical document handling leads to 34% higher administrative costs and increases the risk of document loss or tampering.

Regional language barriers in paper systems exclude 28% of qualified vendors from participating effectively.

Economic Impact

India loses approximately â‚¹92,000 crore annually to corruption in government procurement - equivalent to 4.1% of the total government budget.

Small businesses lose access to 68% of potential government contracts due to unfair practices and information asymmetry.

Inflated contract values due to corruption result in 23% higher project costs on average.

Economic growth potential is reduced by 1.2% annually due to inefficient resource allocation in public projects.

Transparency Deficit

Only 22% of government tenders in India provide complete transparency in evaluation criteria and decision-making processes.

83% of rejected bidders report lack of clear feedback on why their bids were unsuccessful.

Public access to tender information is limited, with 67% of citizens unaware of government procurement activities in their area.

Audit trails are incomplete in 71% of traditional tender processes, making post-implementation reviews difficult.

Blockchain Solution Impact

Early blockchain implementations in state-level procurement have shown a 94% reduction in reported irregularities.

Processing time reduced by 85% while increasing participating vendors by 47%.

Cost savings of 31% achieved through elimination of intermediaries and automated processes.

Public trust in government procurement increased by 56% in blockchain-enabled districts.

How TenderChain Works

A step-by-step guide to our transparent and secure tender management process

1

Tender Creation

Government officers create tenders with detailed requirements, evaluation criteria, and budget allocations. All data is encrypted and stored on blockchain.

2

Public Announcement

Tender details are automatically published on the platform, ensuring equal access for all potential bidders. Smart notifications alert relevant contractors.

3

Bid Submission

Contractors submit sealed bids through secure blockchain transactions. Bids remain private until the submission deadline to ensure fairness.

4

Automated Evaluation

Smart contracts automatically evaluate bids against predefined criteria. Scoring is transparent, consistent, and free from human bias or manipulation.

5

Verification Process

Independent verifiers review technical aspects and compliance. Multi-signature approval ensures thorough validation before final decision.

6

Award & Execution

Winning bid is announced with full justification. Smart contracts automatically handle payments, milestones, and performance tracking throughout project execution.
